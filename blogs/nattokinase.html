Nattokinase: The Natural Enzyme Supporting Cardiovascular Wellness

Updated July 2026 6 min read Aiva Wellness Editorial Team

Long before it became a supplement-aisle staple, nattokinase was simply breakfast — a sticky spoonful of fermented soybeans eaten across Japan for over a thousand years. Today, this quiet enzyme is one of the most talked-about natural allies for a healthy heart.

What Exactly Is Nattokinase?

Nattokinase is an enzyme extracted from natto, a traditional Japanese food made by fermenting boiled soybeans with the bacterium Bacillus subtilis. During fermentation, the bacteria produce this potent enzyme, which has been consumed safely as part of the Japanese diet for centuries.

In supplement form, nattokinase is standardised and concentrated so you get a consistent, measured dose without needing to eat natto itself — which, admittedly, has a distinct smell and texture that isn't for everyone.

Why Nattokinase Is Linked to Heart Health

Healthy circulation depends on blood that flows freely through your vessels. Nattokinase belongs to a class of enzymes known as fibrinolytic enzymes — compounds that support the body's natural process of breaking down fibrin, a protein involved in blood clot formation. This is the main reason nattokinase has drawn attention from researchers and wellness practitioners studying cardiovascular support.

  • Supports healthy blood flow — by assisting the body's own fibrin-clearing processes.
  • Complements a heart-conscious lifestyle — best used alongside regular movement, a balanced diet, and routine check-ups.
  • May support already-normal blood pressure — an area of ongoing scientific interest.
  • Naturally derived — sourced from a fermented food with a long history of dietary use in Japan.

"A healthy heart isn't built in a single supplement — it's built in daily habits. Nattokinase is one small, natural piece of that larger picture."

How Nattokinase Is Typically Used

Nattokinase is usually taken as a capsule, ideally on an empty stomach so the enzyme can be absorbed efficiently before food intake slows digestion. Because it's a fermentation-derived enzyme rather than a synthetic compound, it fits comfortably into a daily wellness routine for most healthy adults.

That said, enzyme potency and appropriate use can vary from person to person — especially for anyone already on blood-thinning medication or managing a diagnosed heart condition.

This article is for general educational purposes and is not medical advice. Nattokinase may interact with blood-thinning medications and is not suitable for everyone. Please consult your doctor before starting any new supplement, particularly if you are pregnant, on medication, or managing an existing health condition. Always follow the dosage on the product label.

Choosing a Quality Nattokinase Supplement

Not all nattokinase capsules are created equal. Potency is typically measured in Fibrinolytic Units (FU), and quality matters just as much as quantity. When choosing a supplement, look for:

  • Standardised potency — so every capsule delivers a consistent, verifiable amount of active enzyme.
  • No unnecessary fillers or binders — a clean capsule means nothing dilutes the active ingredient.
  • Third-party tested manufacturing — for purity, safety, and consistency batch after batch.

The Takeaway

Nattokinase is a compelling example of how traditional foods can inform modern wellness routines. Rooted in centuries of Japanese dietary history and studied for its role in supporting healthy circulation, it's a natural addition worth discussing with your doctor — especially if cardiovascular wellness is already on your radar.
